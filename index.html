Aegean Cruises

Academic Group Project

Background

The premises of this assignment were to create an excursions website for an imaginary cruiseline, with the goal of improving the target audience's satisfaction of those excursions. Our assigned audience was teenagers (ages 12-18). The excursions themselves could not be changed.

Concept

Analysis of demographics, psychographics, personas, and context scenarios led us to consider the elements of friendship and sharing as catalysts for satisfaction. When the audience decides how satisfied they were with an excursion, it is often in hindsight as a memory. Making friends, sharing photos, posting to social media sites, and seeing an influx of reactions from their own personal networks allows the impression to last long after the excursion is over.

The Result

We designed the site to make it easy to integrate friends into the planning and selection of excursions. The website aims to encourage people to share their experiences, both during and after the excursions.

Skills Used

Lucidchart, Adobe Illustrator

Narrative

Hackathon Project

Background

I attended a StartUp Weekend event at my university to participate as a UX Designer and Frontend Developer. The theme of the weekend was to create an application that fosters educational growth or benefit. We were given 53 hours to create a project.

Concept

Having two former teachers on our team, we listened to their past experiences about what they wished they had when they were teaching. We narrowed it down to the subject of creative writing, an important but sometimes overlooked subject. We wanted to enhance the writing experience but still leave the creativity and freedom to the author.

The Result

Upon visiting the site, a random prompt paired with an image is generated for the user. The user types their narrative in the text editor and can refer to helpers on the side if they need more inspiration. They can then publish their story to a community feed.

Skills Used

HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery

InfoCrafts

Personal Project

Background

I have been a fan of Blizzard Entertainment's StarCraft franchise for more than half my life. To improve my skills, I often watch YouTube videos created by my favorite video game personality, Sean "Day[9]" Plott. I, and many others who have seen Sean's videos, all recognize the quality of insightful content he provides when teaching people how to become better gamers. However, his videos are somewhat long to account for all of the information.

Concept

Because a lot of the concepts in StarCraft are processes, memorization tactics, and overall very interactive, I thought the best way to summarize Seans video's would be to make visualizations. The game is very mentally stimulating, so I believed presenting the ideas in a visual way would register with a lot of gamers.

The Result

My visualizations are designed to be quickly read as memory joggers and supplementals to Sean's videos. I also wanted to enhance the content itself by using encoding, visual hierarchy, color, and shape associations to make absorbing the information easy.
